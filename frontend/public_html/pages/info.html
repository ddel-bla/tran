Introduction

Pong is one of the earliest video games in history, created in 1972. It is a digital table tennis game where players control paddles to hit a ball and score points. The game can be played with up to 4 players simultaneously, making it a fun and competitive experience for everyone.

How to Play

  • The ball moves automatically across the screen.
  • Each player controls a paddle, moving it up and down to hit the ball.
  • When the ball hits a paddle, it bounces in the opposite direction.
  • A player scores a point if their opponent fails to return the ball.
  • The game ends when a player reaches the set number of points (usually 11 or 21).
  • In multiplayer mode (up to 4 players), players compete in teams or individually, depending on the game mode.

Boosts

Boosts are special power-ups that enhance your gameplay. Choose wisely to gain an advantage over your opponent!

  • Speed Boost: Increases your paddle's movement speed, allowing for quicker reactions.
  • Power Boost: Makes your shots stronger, causing the ball to travel faster.
  • Defense Boost: Temporarily enlarges your paddle, making it easier to block shots.

Tournament Mode

Tournament mode allows you to play with multiple players in a structured competition. Up to 4 players can participate simultaneously, and the game supports both single-elimination and round-robin formats.

  • You can register between 4 and 8 players.
  • Matches are generated automatically through matchmaking or can be assigned manually.
  • Players advance through a single-elimination bracket or round-robin system.
  • The last player standing is crowned the tournament champion.

Tips to Win

  • Try to hit the ball at angles to make it harder for your opponent to return.
  • React quickly and maintain control of your paddle.
  • Learn to anticipate your opponent's movements.
  • Stay calm and don't panic if you're losing.
  • In multiplayer mode, communicate with your teammate (if playing in teams) to cover more ground.
